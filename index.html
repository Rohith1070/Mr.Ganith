FACTORS that can lead to MATH ANXIETY.


NEGATIVE PAST EXPERIENCES:

Previous negative experiences with math,
such as struggling to understand concepts
or receiving poor grades,can create a sense
of failure,anxietyy,and a fear of math among
students.


PRESSURE AND EXPECTATIONS

The pressure to excel in math,driven by
high expectations from parents,teachers,
or peers,can instill anxiety and fear of
making mistakes or falling ahort of
expectations.

PERCEPTION OF DIFFICULTY

The perception of math as a challenging
subject and the belief of lacking natural
inclination or ability can contribute to a fear
of math and a belief in inevitable failure
among students

LACK OF UNDERSTANDING AND CONFIDENCE

Struggling with math concepts or lacking
confidence can lead to fear,hindering
progress and reinforcing anxiety by creating
a sense of inability to understand or solve
problems.

FEAR OF JUDGEMENT AND EMBARRASSMENT

Math,taught publicly in classrooms,can fuel
anxiety as students fear judgemment and
embarrassment for making mistakes,
dreading being perceived as unintelligent
or incompetent

INEFFECTIVE TEACHING METHODS

Inadequate teaching methods or lack of
indiividualized instruction can amplify the
challenge and overwhelm of math,leaving
students feeling lost or left behind,
fostering a fear of the subject.
